★ For Agencies

Heist for agencies

Manage unlimited client brands from one login. Each brand gets its own Brain, voice, audience, calendar, and analytics — with zero cross-contamination. Scale accounts without scaling headcount.

The agency math problem

You have 12 clients. Each one needs 5 posts a week, across 3-4 platforms. That's roughly 200 pieces of content a month. At even 15 minutes per post, that's 50 hours — more than a full work week, just on content production, before any strategy or client work.

You've tried the obvious stacks. ChatGPT + Buffer. Jasper + Hootsuite. Notion + Canva + everything else. Each client gets their own tangled setup. Each handoff between team members loses context. Each new hire takes weeks to learn where the brand voice lives for each account.

The problem isn't the tools individually — it's that none of them were built to handle multiple brands as a first-class concept. You're the integration layer, and it doesn't scale.

Three agency pains, three fixes

Pain: Brand voice chaos

12 clients means 12 different voices. New team members spend weeks learning each one. Voice drift is constant.

Pain: Fragmented reporting

Every client needs a monthly report. You pull metrics from 4 different tools per client. 48 manual pulls a month.

Pain: Stack cost per client

Scheduler + generator + analytics = $50-120 per client per month. At 12 clients that's $600-1440 in tool costs alone.

How Heist Agency fixes each one

Fix 1: One Brain per client, all in one login

Each client gets their own 10-layer Brain — separate brand profile, audience personas, voice examples, memory facts, and performance history. New team members can switch between clients with a single click and instantly generate content that matches the correct voice. Zero cross-contamination between brands.

Fix 2: Per-brand analytics, unified dashboard

See performance per client, per platform, per campaign — in one view. Every report is a screenshot away. Filter by date range, platform, or campaign type. Export to PDF for client monthlies.

Fix 3: One subscription for everything

Heist Agency at $199/mo replaces the entire multi-client stack. Generator, scheduler, brand memory, analytics — all in one tool, for unlimited brands. Per-client cost: $16/mo at 12 clients, $6/mo at 30 clients.

What agencies actually do with Heist

The onboarding play. New client signs. You sit down once, configure their Brain (voice, audience, example content, never-list), and that's onboarding done. Every post after that draws from that Brain automatically. No more "who's writing the post and do they know the voice?" meetings.

The team delegation play. Junior team members can generate posts for any client because the Brain handles voice matching. Your review time drops because first drafts are already on-brand. Senior time goes to strategy, not line-editing.

The client reporting play. End of month, pull the analytics view for the client. Export PDF. Send. Done in 5 minutes instead of 60.

The proposal play. When pitching new clients, show them the Brain setup screen. Explain that their voice, audience, and performance data will live in one place forever. It's a differentiator against agencies that just "use ChatGPT."

Recommended tier for agencies

Heist Agency — $199/mo. Unlimited brands, unlimited posts, unlimited campaigns, team seats, all platforms, premium AI models. Compare to Hootsuite Team ($249/mo), Sprout Social ($249-499/mo), or a multi-tool stack ($600-1400/mo across 12 clients).

The math at scale

A typical 12-client agency running Buffer + ChatGPT + Notion + analytics tooling pays roughly $800-1200/mo in per-client software, plus 30-50 hours a week on content logistics (copy-pasting, switching contexts, syncing tools).

The same agency on Heist Agency at $199/mo saves roughly $600-1000/mo in tool costs and 15-25 hours a week in logistics time. At $75/hr loaded cost for agency time, that's $4,500-7,500/mo in recovered billable capacity. The subscription pays for itself several times over before you count any client growth.

More importantly: your agency stops being capped by content production time and starts being capped by how many clients you can sell. That's a much better ceiling.
