Heist for SaaS companies

SaaS content has to do three jobs at once: build thought leadership (brand), drive product awareness (demand gen), and create assets your sales team can use.

Why SaaS companies struggle with content

SaaS content has to do three jobs at once: build thought leadership (brand), drive product awareness (demand gen), and create assets your sales team can use. Most content tools only handle one of those jobs. Heist's Brain is architected to generate all three modes from the same brand voice without sounding schizophrenic.

Three pains, three fixes

Marketing team of 1-3 people

Early-stage SaaS marketing is a small team juggling blog, social, email, and paid. Content is the bottleneck because it can't be automated without losing quality.

Generic thought leadership sounds like everyone else

Every B2B SaaS blog reads the same — "5 best practices for X." Generic AI makes this worse. You need content that actually reflects your founder's take, not industry-average mush.

Founder content is the highest-leverage but hardest to maintain

Founder-led content converts 5-10x better than brand content. But founders don't have time to write 10 posts a week. They have time to dictate an idea and have someone else write it.

How Heist fixes each one

Fix 1: Founder Brain + Brand Brain

Heist Agency tier lets you set up multiple Brains — one for your founder voice (punchy, opinionated, personal) and one for your brand voice (polished, educational, product-focused). Your founder dictates ideas into the Founder Brain, your marketer writes product content via the Brand Brain. Different voices, one subscription.

Fix 2: Campaign-level content generation

Launching a new feature? Heist generates a full launch campaign: tease, announcement, customer use cases, comparison to alternatives, founder story about why you built it, and retention check-ins. 20+ posts across platforms from one brief.

Fix 3: Long-form blog generation in your voice

Heist Founder+ generates SEO blog content in the same voice as your social posts — critical for SaaS because blog-to-social voice drift kills trust. A reader who follows you on LinkedIn and clicks to your blog should feel like they're still reading the same company.

Plays SaaS companies run with Heist

  • The founder-led play. Founder dictates raw thoughts into voice memos → marketer edits into brand-voice posts using Heist. 30 minutes a week of founder time produces 5-7 high-conversion posts.
  • The feature launch play. New feature → Heist generates tease, reveal, use case, comparison, and retention campaigns. Full sequence in one session.
  • The "built in public" play. Weekly behind-the-scenes posts about what you shipped, what broke, what you learned. Heist turns three bullet points into a full post.
  • The comparison page play. For every competitor, Heist generates an honest comparison page (like the one you're reading). Massive SEO value.

Frequently asked questions

What's the best content tool for SaaS marketing?

For SaaS teams that need both blog content and multi-platform social in one voice, Heist Founder ($99/mo) or Agency ($199/mo) is purpose-built. Alternatives like Jasper handle blog content but require separate tools for social scheduling and voice consistency.

Can Heist handle founder-led content?

Yes — Agency tier lets you run separate Brains for founder voice and brand voice, so founder content feels personal and brand content stays polished. Both in one subscription.

Does Heist do technical content?

For developer-facing content, yes — Heist's Brain can be tuned for technical voice via example content. For deeply technical tutorials, you'll probably still want a human writer, but Heist is great for dev-focused marketing (launch posts, how-it-works threads, changelog announcements).

How does Heist compare to Jasper or Writesonic for SaaS?

Jasper and Writesonic are stronger on long-form SEO blog posts. Heist is stronger on multi-platform social + brand memory + scheduling. Many SaaS teams use both — or settle on Heist for the voice consistency across blog and social.
